E-commerce Mobile App Redesign

Transforming user experience to increase conversion rates

Back to Portfolio

Project Overview

I was tasked with redesigning a struggling e-commerce mobile app that had low conversion rates and poor user engagement. The existing app had a cluttered interface, confusing navigation, and a lengthy checkout process that was causing users to abandon their purchases.

Role

Lead UI/UX Designer

Duration

3 months

Team

2 Developers, 1 PM, 1 Designer

Tools

Figma, Principle, Maze

The Problem

The original app suffered from several critical issues:

  • Conversion rate was only 1.2%, well below industry average of 2.5%
  • Cart abandonment rate was 78%
  • Users struggled to find products due to poor navigation
  • Checkout process had 7 steps, causing user fatigue
  • Mobile responsiveness issues on various screen sizes

Research & Discovery

I conducted comprehensive user research to understand the pain points and identify opportunities for improvement.

1

User Interviews

Conducted 15 interviews with existing users to understand their frustrations and needs.

2

Analytics Review

Analyzed user behavior data to identify drop-off points and problem areas.

3

Competitive Analysis

Studied successful e-commerce apps to identify best practices and opportunities.

4

Usability Testing

Tested the existing app with 10 users to document specific usability issues.

Design Solution

Based on research insights, I developed a comprehensive redesign strategy focusing on three key areas:

Enhanced Search & Discovery

Implemented smart search with filters, categories, and visual product browsing to help users find items quickly.

Streamlined Checkout

Reduced checkout from 7 steps to 3, with guest checkout option and multiple payment methods.

Personalized Experience

Added wishlist, recommendation engine, and user account features to increase engagement.

Key Learnings

This project reinforced several important UX principles:

  • User research is invaluable: Direct user feedback revealed issues that analytics alone couldn't identify
  • Simplicity wins: Reducing complexity in the checkout process had the biggest impact on conversions
  • Iterate and test: Continuous testing and refinement led to better outcomes than a single big redesign
  • Mobile-first approach: Designing for mobile constraints improved the overall user experience
