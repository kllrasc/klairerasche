Healthcare App User Research

Designing accessible patient experiences through comprehensive research

Back to Portfolio

Project Overview

I led a comprehensive user research initiative to redesign a patient portal for a major healthcare provider. The project focused on improving accessibility, usability, and patient satisfaction while ensuring compliance with healthcare regulations.

Role

Lead UX Researcher & Designer

Duration

6 months

Team

2 Researchers, 3 Designers, 4 Developers

Tools

Figma, UserTesting, Lookback, Miro

The Challenge

The existing patient portal faced significant usability and accessibility challenges:

  • Patient satisfaction scores were at 2.8/5, well below industry standards
  • 65% of users over 65 couldn't complete basic tasks independently
  • The app failed multiple WCAG 2.1 accessibility standards
  • Support ticket volume was increasing by 15% monthly
  • Only 23% of patients were actively using digital features

Research Methodology

I designed a multi-phase research approach to understand diverse patient needs and healthcare contexts.

Patient Interviews

Conducted 35 in-depth interviews across different age groups, health conditions, and tech literacy levels to understand varied patient needs.

Usability Testing

Performed moderated testing sessions with 25 patients, including accessibility testing with assistive technologies.

Analytics Analysis

Analyzed user behavior data and support tickets to identify common pain points and usage patterns.

Healthcare Provider Interviews

Interviewed 15 healthcare providers to understand workflow integration and clinical requirements.

Key User Personas

Research revealed three primary user groups with distinct needs and challenges:

Margaret, 72 - Primary Care Patient

Limited tech experience, multiple chronic conditions

Goals: Easy access to test results, appointment scheduling, medication reminders

Challenges: Small text, complex navigation, remembering passwords

Sarah, 34 - Working Parent

Tech-savvy, manages family healthcare

Goals: Quick access to family information, mobile-first experience, integrated communication

Challenges: Time constraints, managing multiple family member accounts

James, 45 - Accessibility Needs

Uses screen reader, frequent hospital visits

Goals: Screen reader compatibility, keyboard navigation, clear information hierarchy

Challenges: Poor accessibility implementation, inconsistent interaction patterns

Key Research Insights

The research phase revealed critical insights that shaped the design strategy:

  • Age-inclusive design benefits everyone: Larger fonts and simplified navigation improved usability for all age groups
  • Trust is paramount in healthcare: Clear privacy indicators and secure design patterns increased user confidence
  • Context matters: Patients often use the app during stressful medical situations, requiring extra-clear communication
  • Accessibility drives adoption: Removing barriers led to 45% increase in active user engagement

Implementation & Testing

The rollout included extensive testing and validation with real patients:

  • Pilot testing: Beta tested with 50 patients across different demographic groups
  • Accessibility auditing: Third-party accessibility review and certification
  • Gradual rollout: Phased implementation with continuous user feedback collection
  • Staff training: Comprehensive training for healthcare providers and support staff

Future Research Opportunities

Based on this research, we've identified several areas for continued investigation:

  • Voice interface integration for hands-free interaction
  • Caregiver access and family account management
  • Integration with wearable health devices
  • Multilingual support and cultural considerations
