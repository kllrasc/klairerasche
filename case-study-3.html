Player Onboarding Experience

Designing engaging tutorials through comprehensive player research

Back to Portfolio

Project Overview

I led a comprehensive player research initiative to redesign the onboarding experience for a strategy mobile game. The project focused on improving tutorial completion, player engagement, and retention while ensuring players learned essential game mechanics.

Role

Lead Game UX Researcher & Designer

Duration

6 months

Team

2 Game Designers, 3 Developers, 1 Narrative Designer

Tools

Figma, Unity, Playtesting Tools, Analytics

The Challenge

The existing onboarding experience faced significant usability and engagement challenges:

  • Tutorial completion rate was at 35%, well below industry standards of 70%
  • 85% of new players couldn't understand core game mechanics after tutorial
  • The tutorial was too long and overwhelming for mobile players
  • Player drop-off rate was 60% during the first 5 minutes
  • Only 15% of players were actively engaging with advanced features after onboarding

Research Methodology

I designed a multi-phase research approach to understand diverse player learning styles and onboarding preferences.

Player Interviews

Conducted 40 in-depth interviews across different gaming experience levels and age groups to understand varied learning preferences.

Gameplay Testing

Performed moderated testing sessions with 30 new players, recording their onboarding journey and identifying pain points.

Analytics Analysis

Analyzed player behavior data and drop-off points to identify common failure patterns in the tutorial flow.

Competitive Research

Studied 20 successful mobile games to understand onboarding best practices and innovative tutorial approaches.

Key Player Personas

Research revealed three primary player groups with distinct learning styles and challenges:

Alex, 16 - Casual Mobile Player

Limited strategy game experience, plays during commute

Goals: Quick, fun gameplay without complex mechanics, easy controls

Challenges: Gets overwhelmed by lengthy tutorials, prefers learning by doing

Maria, 28 - Experienced Gamer

Strategy game veteran, wants to master all features

Goals: Quick access to advanced features, comprehensive understanding of mechanics

Challenges: Impatient with basic tutorials, wants to skip to complex strategies

Robert, 45 - Time-Constrained Player

Busy professional, plays during breaks

Goals: Efficient learning, clear progress indicators, ability to pause anytime

Challenges: Limited time slots, needs tutorials that respect time constraints

Key Research Insights

The research phase revealed critical insights that shaped the tutorial strategy:

  • Show, don't tell: Interactive tutorials were 3x more effective than text-based explanations
  • Respect player time: Breaking tutorials into digestible 30-second chunks improved completion
  • Context is everything: Players learned better when tutorials connected to immediate gameplay goals
  • Progress visibility matters: Clear indicators of tutorial progress increased completion rates by 40%

Implementation & Testing

The rollout included extensive testing and validation with real players:

  • Pilot testing: Beta tested with 100 players across different skill levels
  • A/B testing: Compared multiple tutorial approaches to optimize effectiveness
  • Gradual rollout: Phased implementation with continuous player feedback collection
  • Analytics monitoring: Real-time tracking of completion rates and engagement metrics

Future Enhancement Opportunities

Based on this research, we've identified several areas for continued improvement:

  • Adaptive tutorials that adjust to player skill level in real-time
  • AI-powered hints system for players who get stuck
  • Social features allowing players to share tutorial progress
  • Voice-guided tutorials for accessibility and hands-free learning
